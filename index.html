In simple terms

How is this different from a regular RAG?

Both look at your existing content before answering. The difference is what "evidence" means to each one.

Traditional RAG

Searches for similar text

Splits documents into chunks and retrieves the ones that sound closest to your question.

If the answer needs to connect two facts that live in different chunks, the model has to fill the gap itself — which is exactly where it can hallucinate.

This system (KAG)

Walks a real graph

Your question is matched to one real node. A deterministic algorithm — not the LLM — walks the actual edges from there: symptom → cause → action.

If a step in that chain doesn't exist, or the question is ambiguous, the agent says so — or asks for the missing detail — instead of inventing a relationship.
